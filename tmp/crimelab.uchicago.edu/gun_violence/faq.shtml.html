Design Competition FAQ

Do organizations responding to the RFLOI need to be based in Chicago?
No, we only require that the organization fulfill the requirements listed in the RFLOI and that the target population (kids, schools, neighborhoods, etc) be based in Chicago.

Do organizations responding have to be tax-exempt or may private/for-profit organizations apply?
Any organization that fulfills the requirements spelled out in the RFLOI may apply. One cannot apply as an individual.

Are organizations allowed to submit more than one letter of interest?
Organizations (and groups of organizations) may submit as many separate letters of interest as they have good ideas for promising interventions.

How many winners will be selected?
That depends on the estimated cost of the top choice and the Crime Lab’s sense of the potential external fundraising environment. We will select as many winners as seem most likely to be feasible to implement given our target fundraising goal.

Why is the evaluation design so specific?
This sort of evaluation provides the most rigorous scientific evidence about program impact. Other types of evaluations provide less reliable findings. Given the life-and-death consequences associated with trying to identify the most effective violence intervention strategies, it is essential to deploy the most rigorous evaluation strategies.

Is the RFLOI/Design Competition the only mechanism for working with the Crime Lab on a program evaluation?
In some cases, organizations may consult with or seek advice from the Crime Lab independent of the RFLOI.

Our organization lacks the capacity or expertise to perform extensive statistical analysis. Are we still eligible to apply for the RFLOI?
The main RFLOI selection criterion is that an applicant can successfully implement a well-designed intervention amenable to rigorous evaluation. Organizations that can implement such interventions can receive technical assistance regarding data analysis, determining the required sample size, and similar matters.

Does selection guarantee funding?
No. However, the Crime Lab will work with selected organizations to try to obtain funding from private sources.

Our interventions address key risk-factors for youth violence such as improving school performance or paid employment, but we do not directly address violence itself.
Interventions that successfully address key risk-factors for violence such as schooling or paid employment are competitive for RFLOI funding.

Will the Crime Lab ask us to deny services to some clients to facilitate evaluation?
No. Successful evaluation designs will serve all youth who can be served within the available resources (should the Crime Lab’s external fundraising efforts be successful so that a program is actually funded and implemented).
